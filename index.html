About YDSA

The future is ours to win

Democratic Socialists of America (DSA) is the largest socialist organization in America, and YDSA is the Youth & campus wing of that org. DSA has over 66,000 members, including congresswomen Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez & Rashida Tlaib. As socialists, we believe that:

  • The economy should be run democratically by workers
  • The liberation of all oppressed peoples requires the abolition of Capitalism
  • The US imperial empire must be dismantled globally
  • We can win these victories through working class struggle!

Our Organizing

UCLA YDSA organizes on a number of different fronts to build student and worker power at the university, including electoral campaigns, union support, and direct action. See some highlights below.

Labor Organizing

UCLA YDSA is committed to strengthening the working class.

We've supported strikes, educated undergraduates, made close connections with unions like UC-AFT & UAW 2865 on campus, and have even gotten our own members to join union rank and file!

Bernie Canvassing

UCLA YDSA has campaigned for Bernie Sanders on and off campus.

Throughout the 2020 Democratic Primary, we have supported the Bernie campaign as much as we can. We've canvassed at least twice a week across campus (you may have seen our table in front of YRL), and have also sent teams of up to 25 people to canvass in locations across Los Angeles.

Direct Action

UCLA YDSA has become a fixture at local protests & actions.

From standing against Donald Trump Jr.'s harmful appearance at Moore Hall to supporting the Iranian people's struggle against America's imperialist wars, we have turned out to defend our communities and present a strong left-wing voice at public actions.

Community Events

UCLA YDSA is creating long-lasting community on campus.

We've held Democratic Debate Watch Parties, Political Education Sessions, Reading Groups, and even Socialist Fundragers in an effort to foster a leftist social community at UCLA.

Coordinating Committee

UCLA YDSA is lead by a democratically elected Coordinating Committee, which establishes chapter priorities, organizes meetings, manages funds, and ensures that the chapter grows and develops in healthy ways.
